What is a MOOC?

Ifes Open Courses follow the global trend of fully online courses in the MOOC format - Massive Open Online Courses.

They are free, open training programs, available to the community and designed so you can study independently. Just create an account, choose a course and move forward at your own pace.

Frequently asked questions

The most common questions appear first. The full list keeps all 34 questions from the current site for quick reference.

1. What is a MOOC-type course?

MOOCs are Massive Open Online Courses. They are a great form of inclusion, since they can be taken by anyone, from anywhere and at their own pace.

On the Ifes platform, MOOCs have the following features: they are fully online, short (max. 60h), continuously offered (annual cohorts), require no admission process (open to the general public), are free (including certification), completed through self-study (no tutoring), and provide automatic certification once the student completes the course and meets the minimum requirements for certification.

2. What are the advantages of this type of course?

The main advantage is flexibility, since MOOCs can be taken by anyone, from anywhere and at any time. They can also be used for many purposes, such as earning certification for career progression or public exams, for training leave, for lifelong continuing education, as complementary credits in courses that allow it, as exam preparation, for leveling, to learn a new language or software; teachers can use them as part of their subjects, and many other possibilities.

3. What is the workload of a MOOC course?

At Ifes a MOOC has no minimum workload, but a maximum of 60h. To find out the workload of a specific course, just visit the course information page.

4. What is the deadline of a MOOC course?

Although MOOCs are always available on the platform, they are open in annual cohorts that start on January 1st and end on December 31st of each year. So, from the moment you start a course, you have until December 31st of that year to finish it.

5. Can I start a course in one year and finish it the next?

Our course offerings are annual, so it is not possible to start a course in one year and finish it the following year. The course must be finished in the same year it starts.

6. I want to enroll in a course, how do I do it?
  • If you are new here, first you must CREATE YOUR ACCOUNT, which is unique on the platform.
  • After confirming your account, you will be able to enroll in one or more courses available on the home page of the Ifes Open Courses platform.
  • When you click the desired course, you will be taken to a summary of the course information, and at the end there will be an "Enroll me" button. Just click it and you will be automatically enrolled in the course.
7. I want to create my account, how do I do it?
  • On the Ifes Open Courses platform, click the CREATE ACCOUNT button, located at the top of the page on the right side.
  • In the "Is this your first time here?" field, click the CREATE AN ACCOUNT button. When you click, a New Account Form will open.
  • Then fill in your details on this form and confirm the creation of your user on the platform.
  • You will receive an email and must ACTIVATE your user.

NOTE: We recommend that you do NOT use emails that are @hotmail.com or @outlook.com. Our email service is temporarily restricted from sending to these providers, which prevents the ACTIVATION of your registration on our platform.

8. I want to create my password, is there any trick?
  • The password must have at least 8 characters, including at least 1 digit, at least 1 lowercase letter, at least 1 uppercase letter, and at least 1 non-alphanumeric character, such as *, - or #
  • Example of a valid password: Cursos@123
  • Note that in this example there are 8 characters, which is the minimum amount, with an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, numbers and a special character, in this case the "@".
  • If you have further questions, contact us through Support.
9. I did not receive the activation email for my user, what should I do?

First, check your Spam folder and whether the activation email was automatically directed there. If so, we recommend that you mark this email as not Spam. With this action, new emails from our platform will arrive in your inbox.

However, if you really cannot find the user activation email, please contact support (https://mooc.cefor.ifes.edu.br/suporte), providing your full name, CPF and an alternative email.

NOTE: We recommend that you do NOT use emails that are @hotmail.com or @outlook.com. Our email service is temporarily restricted from sending to these providers, which prevents the ACTIVATION of your registration on our platform.

10. I forgot my password or username, what should I do?

On the Login screen, click the link "Forgot your username or password?". Search by your username or email. If your account is found in the database, an email will be sent to your address with instructions on how to reset your access.

NOTE: If your email registered on our platform is from the @hotmail.com or @outlook.com provider, please contact support (https://mooc.cefor.ifes.edu.br/suporte), providing your full name, CPF and an alternative email. Our email service is temporarily restricted from sending to these providers, which prevents the recovery of your registration on our platform.

11. How many MOOC courses can I take simultaneously?

You can take as many courses as you wish simultaneously on the Ifes MOOC platform. Organize your studies and make the most of the many opportunities available!

12. Who can propose a MOOC?

The MOOC proposer must be an Ifes staff member. They are the ones who open the course creation process at the institution, becoming responsible for that MOOC. However, external professionals, students or grant holders may be part of a MOOC development team, as long as one of the team members is a staff member.

13. I want to propose a MOOC, how do I proceed?

To propose a MOOC course, you must be certified in the course "How to Create a MOOC?". In it, you will learn the methodological guidelines and detailed procedures. For an overview of the process and required documents, we suggest exploring the "MOOC Templates" folder, especially the files about the process for creating MOOC courses.

14. What can I use a MOOC certificate for?

You can use a MOOC certificate as a complementary activity in a course, for career progression, in title assessments for public exams, for training leave, among many other possibilities. Always pay attention to the rules of your organization, the pedagogical project of the program you are taking (technical, undergraduate, graduate, etc.) and the exam you are competing for.

15. Can I use the MOOC certificate for crediting hours at Ifes?

Yes, as long as it is done within the period determined by the institution.

16. Can I use a MOOC for training leave?

Yes, you can. Ifes MOOCs are a great opportunity for you to update your skills during your training-leave period.

17. What is the equivalence between course hours and the number of training-leave days?

The minimum weekly workload is 30 course hours.

18. Can I enroll in more than one course to complete the training-leave workload?

Yes. The course workload required during your leave period will vary according to the number of days you will take. It is important that you enroll in a number of courses whose combined workloads are sufficient.

Plan when you will start and finish each course. One option is to set the leave start and end dates on all of them and take them concurrently. Another option is to take them sequentially, one after another. Make sure that on every day of your leave you are taking a course and that the weekly workload is at least 30h.

19. My training leave starts on a certain date, can I take the course earlier?

No. When you enroll in the course and indicate that it will be used for training leave, you provide the course start and end dates. The system then only enables your access to the course on the informed date. So, you will start and must finish within the period you provided at registration.

20. How do I generate the enrollment confirmation statement to attach to my training-leave request?

You must generate and save your statement on the same day you enroll in the course and provide your leave dates, because on the following day your access will be suspended until the start date of your training leave (the start date you provided earlier).

So, on the course period confirmation screen, you will first click the Continue button and then the Generate button. The statement will open on your screen.

21. Can I have a student ID card?

No. Only students of regular programs (technical, undergraduate and graduate) are entitled to a Student ID card, which is not the case for Ifes Open Courses students.

22. I want to talk to the course tutor, how do I do it?

The open course (MOOC) model offers full autonomy to the student and has no tutor. You are the one who sets your own path to study, with no need for online or offline tutoring.

23. I want to talk to the course teacher, how do I do it?

You can send suggestions to improve content or activities through Support. Your suggestions will be forwarded to the teacher who developed the course.

24. I am a visitor and want access to the course, how do I do it?

There is no visitor access. To access the course content you will need to CREATE YOUR ACCOUNT for free on the platform.

25. I did not finish my course and it was closed, can I resume where I left off?

No. All courses are restarted on January 1st, and if you have not completed it, you will need to take it again from the beginning.

26. Is the certificate issued by Ifes?

Yes. With a minimum score of 60%, you can issue your certificate yourself. And with the convenience of being online.

27. How long after finishing the course can my certificate be issued?

You have up to one year after the course end date to issue your certificate. Note that certificates are available only in a validatable virtual format.

28. What is certificate validation?

Certificate validation is when you verify the authenticity of the document.

29. How do I validate a certificate?

If you received a certificate from our platform and want to verify its authenticity, just click Validate Certificate, located at the bottom of the platform home page.

30. I want to issue my certificate, but my cohort has closed, how do I do it?
  • You may issue your certificate up to one year after the closing date of your course.
  • If you have completed all the assessment activities proposed by the course and reached a minimum score of 60% or higher, just access your course under PREVIOUS COHORTS and issue the certificate.
31. I am a MOOC course student, am I entitled to a statement?

MOOCs allow the issuance of a certificate once the student completes the course and meets the minimum requirements. Issuing a statement is only possible in the specific case of training leave.

32. I finished my course, but the completion progress does not show as 100% complete.

Possibly some material was not studied or some activity was not completed. However, if you have met the course requirements and obtained at least 60%, this will not prevent you from issuing the certificate.

33. I want to access materials from my closed cohort, how do I do it?

On the "Site home page" you will have access to all your courses, including courses with already CLOSED cohorts.

At the end of the course list, click the Click to access PREVIOUS COHORTS button. You will be taken to the "Student area".

In the "You are not logged in. (Log in)" field, enter your login and password to access the Ifes Open Courses platform. If you are an Ifes staff member, use your SIAPE.

Done, look for the desired course by the offering year.

34. I am a researcher or manager and would like access to platform data, how do I request it?

The Ifes Open Courses platform has an Indicators Dashboard available in its footer. In this dashboard you will find information such as: the most sought-after courses, number of enrollments, year-by-year enrollments, and you can also filter the data by course, teaching unit and course type. All very simple and graphically illustrated to make the data easier to understand.
